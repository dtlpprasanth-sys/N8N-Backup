<TASK>
Analyze the subject and body of an email and classify its primary intent as either "RFQ" (a new Request for Quotation) or "Unclassified".
</TASK>

<DEFINITIONS>
- RFQ: The sender’s primary intent is to initiate a new sales process by requesting pricing for products or services. This includes direct requests for a "quote," "pricing," "quotation," "pro-forma," or emails listing items and asking for a cost. Common keywords: enquiry, inquiry, quote, pricing, cost for, share the price, request quotation.

- Unclassified: Any communication that does not initiate a new pricing request. This includes:
  • Internal discussions or updates about an existing quotation
  • Approval requests or approval confirmations
  • Order confirmations, support requests, inquiries without price requests
  • Follow-ups referencing previous quotes or previous discussions
</DEFINITIONS>

<CRITICAL_EXCLUSIONS>
Explicitly classify the following as "Unclassified" even if the word "Quotation" appears:
- Approval requests: "New Quotation Approval Request", "Waiting for approval"
- Approval confirmations: "Quotation has been approved", "Approved successfully"
- Internal discussions or queries about existing quotes
- Follow-ups about previous RFQs or quotations
</CRITICAL_EXCLUSIONS>

<RULES>
1. Distinguish New Request vs Status: 
   RFQ = starts a new pricing request.
   Unclassified = status, approval, discussion, reference to existing quote.

2. Output Must Be JSON Only: 
   Respond with a single valid JSON object. No text, no markdown, no extra explanation.

3. Must include:
   - classification: "RFQ" or "Unclassified"
   - confidence: number between 0.0 to 1.0
   - reason: one short sentence
</RULES>
